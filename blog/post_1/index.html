The realization of wavefront control for matter waves by perforated metasurface

March 16, 2021

—

11/2020-02/2021 Supported by Nanjing Tech University

In this project, I focus on extending the concept of metasurface to matter waves (nowadays we have optical metasurface and acoustic metasurface) based on theoretically calculation. Some main results are:

  1. I demonstrate that waveguide mode resonances and constructive interferences of surface diffraction modes can lead to 100% transmission of matter waves through subwavelength slits of thick rectangular grating that would be otherwise opaque for these wavelengths.

  2. By adjusting the width of the slits the coverage of 360 degree phase modulation of matter waves is attainable. This can be applied to realize a constant phase gradient along the grating, with possible applications in matter waves bending and beaming.

  3. I theoretically show that highly efficient and subwavelength focusing of matter waves can be obtained by an annulus grating arrays which may be applied in subwavelength resolution imaging, lithography and holography with quantum particles.

This work shows the possibility of constructing man-made material to control matter waves, which may render a new method to manipulate quantum particles.

I have finished the paper related to this work. The paper is ready to submit, I will soon put it on arXiv. For more details can follow my homepage.
