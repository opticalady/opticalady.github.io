Application of high efficiency porous and slotted metasurface to optical polarization and phase control

March 15, 2021

—

08/2020-09/2020 Supported by the National Natural Science Foundation of China and Nanjin Tech University

In this project we need to realize high efficiency polarization and wavefront control of light by porous and slotted metasurface. The prototype of this kind of metasurface has been studied in [1,2]. In this project, I mainly focus on wavefront control and find highly-efficient wavefront bending in optical regime is attainable by a single-layer perforated metasurface (plasmonic metasurface, free-standing). Paper on the work has been published [3] (see PDF). Some key points are:

  1. A phase shift of 180 degree can be obtained by changing the position of the groove (see figure (a)-(d) above). This greatly simplifies the design process and improves the efficiency.
  2. By changing the cross section of the hole, phase of 180 degree (see figure (e) above) and transmission amplitude (see figure (f) above) can be controlled simultaneously in optical regime.
  3. Pick out three groups of hole parameters to design a super-unit-cell (see figure (g) above). The transmission amplitude of each unit cell is around 0.8 and a phase gradient along the super-unit-cell is attainable (see figure (h) above). Thus, highly-efficient (~60%) wavefront bending in optical regime by a single-layer perforated metasurface can be realized (see figure (a) and (b) below).

Moreover, for some interests, I also extend this kind of metasurface to microwaves frequency regime where the freestanding metasurfaces own almost no loss and can be fabricated easily. The structure has another slot at the opposite side of the groove (see figure (a) below). By simulation, I get the dependence of the transmission phase on the length of the slot (the transmission amplitude can keep high). Based on their relationship, I design a metalens (see figure (b) below) which can realize the focusing of light with high efficiency (see figure (c) and figure (d) below).
